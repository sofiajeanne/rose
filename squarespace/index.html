About

I watch for the sparkle

I'm Sofia-Jeanne — organizational psychologist, relational facilitator, and founder of The Relational School. I work with people at the place where inner change meets outer transformation.

I work with people who've done the inner work — meditation, therapy, personal development — but still find themselves stuck in the same relational patterns. Smart, self-aware people who understand their patterns intellectually but can't seem to break free of them in the moment.

"We're not adding more insight. We're loosening your grip on who you think you have to be."

Through embodied practice — not just conversation — we create real change in how you show up in relationship. How we relate to ourselves shapes how we relate to each other, and that shapes everything we build together.

I hold dual master's degrees from Columbia University and UNC Kenan-Flagler, and I've spent over a decade facilitating circling, shadow work, and embodied inquiry — from week-long retreats around the world to deep one-on-one work.

  • You want your transformation to be embodied, not just intellectual

The Approach

Not more insight — more aliveness

Most approaches to self-improvement take your sense of self for granted. They help you make the room you're in more comfortable. I work differently.

We loosen your relationship to your "self" — the patterns, defenses, and stories you've mistaken for who you are. Through embodied practice, relational experimentation, and building tolerance for the feelings you've been avoiding, you discover something radical: you can change. Not by trying harder, but by holding yourself more loosely.

⚡

Pattern Breaking

Identify the relational loops that keep you stuck, then practice something different — in real time, with real people.

🌊

Nervous System

Learn to regulate your body so you can stay present when things get real — instead of freezing, fleeing, or fighting.

✨

Somatic Awareness

Drop from the story in your head into the wisdom of your body. Sensation is information. Presence is practice.

💛

Relational Practice

Transformation doesn't happen alone. We grow in relationship — by being seen, seeing others, and staying when it's hard.
