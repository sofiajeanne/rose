About

I work with people who've done the inner work — meditation, therapy, personal development — but still find themselves stuck in the same relational patterns. Smart, self-aware people who understand their patterns intellectually but can't seem to break free of them in the moment.

My approach is different. We're not adding more insight. We're loosening your grip on who you think you have to be. Through embodied practice — not just conversation — we create real change in how you show up in relationship.

"The work is simultaneously more radical AND more accessible. Instead of constantly managing your experience, you actually become okay with how it is. Through practical action you find out that your patterns don't have the power you thought they did — and that you can choose, no matter what you're feeling, to take right action in your life and in your relationships."

Who This Is For

  • You've done a lot of spiritual or personal development work, but your relationships still feel hard
  • You can see your patterns clearly, but knowing about them doesn't stop them from running you
  • You're tired of rearranging furniture in the same room — you want to realize you don't have to stay in that room at all
  • You're ready to practice something new in relationship, not just understand it
  • You want your transformation to be embodied, not just intellectual

The Approach

Most approaches to self-improvement take your sense of self for granted. They help you make the room you're in more comfortable. I work differently.

We loosen your relationship to your "self" — the patterns, defenses, and stories you've mistaken for who you are. Through embodied practice, relational experimentation, and building tolerance for the feelings you've been avoiding, you discover something radical: you can change. Not by trying harder, but by holding yourself more loosely.

This is what neuroplasticity feels like. Uncomfortable, sometimes disorienting, and then — free.
